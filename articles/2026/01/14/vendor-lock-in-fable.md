# The Day the Observatory Went Dark: A Fable About Vendor Lock-in
Here is a children’s story based on the events described, with all names anonymized.

---

### The Little Watchers and the Gilded Tower

Once upon a time, in the digital kingdom of Cloudia, there lived a team called the **Little Watchers**.

The Little Watchers had a very important job: they had to make sure the kingdom’s rivers were flowing, the winds were blowing, and the machines were humming. To do this, they rented space in the **Great Gilded Observatory**.

The Great Gilded Observatory was magnificent. It belonged to the **Big Landlord**. It had polished crystal windows, velvet chairs, and golden telescopes. It was the fanciest place in the kingdom to look at the world. The Little Watchers paid a lot of gold coins to stay there because the view was so clear and beautiful.

But one snowy morning in December, the Little Watchers found a note tacked to the front door. It read:

> *"Dear Little Watchers,*
> *We think you are becoming too good at watching things. You are starting to look like a competitor to us. So, we are locking the door. Goodbye!"*

At first, the Little Watchers were confused. "Surely this is a mistake!" they thought. "We are good tenants!" But then, the lights went out. The Great Gilded Observatory locked its gates. For a few scary hours, the Little Watchers couldn't see the rivers, the winds, or the machines. They were flying blind!

They could have stood outside the gate and begged to be let back in. But instead, the Little Watchers took a deep breath. They realized something important: **"It is dangerous to live in a house where the landlord can change the locks whenever he wants."**

So, they picked up their toolboxes and marched down the hill to the **Community Brickyard**.

The Brickyard wasn't as fancy as the Observatory. It didn't have velvet chairs or golden telescopes. It was a place full of loose bricks and simple tools where everyone built things together. It was a bit messy, but it was free, and it belonged to everyone.

As the Little Watchers started building their own simple viewing deck, they realized something magical.

In the old days, they needed the Great Gilded Observatory because *people* had to stare through the telescopes all day long to find problems. They needed the comfy chairs and the clear glass.

But the world had changed. The Little Watchers didn't stare through telescopes anymore. They had built **Magic Robot Helpers** to do the watching for them!

The Magic Robots didn't care about velvet chairs. They didn't care about polished crystal windows. The Robots just needed raw numbers and open space to fly around. In fact, the fancy walls of the Observatory had just been getting in the Robots' way!

The Little Watchers smiled. They realized the Big Landlord had actually done them a favor.

"Thank you for locking us out!" they shouted up at the hill. "We don't need your expensive windows anymore. Our Robots fly better in the open air!"

And so, the Little Watchers learned that sometimes, getting kicked out of a comfortable castle is the best way to learn how to build your own.

---

### The Moral of the Story

1. **Don't rely on just one friend who owns all your toys.** If they take them away, you can't play anymore. It is better to have your own simple toys.
2. **Shiny things aren't always better.** The Gilded Observatory was pretty, but the Community Brickyard was stronger because it couldn't be locked.
3. **Times change.** You used to need a fancy window to look through with your eyes. But now that you have Magic Robots (AI) to do the work, you don't need the window—you just need the data!
