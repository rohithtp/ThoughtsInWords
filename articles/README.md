# ThoughtsInWords

A personal repository for organizing and archiving written thoughts, reflections, and articles.

## Purpose

ThoughtsInWords is a structured space for capturing daily thoughts, ideas, articles, and reflections.

## Organization

Articles are organized by **topic** (tags) rather than chronologically. This aligns with a "digital garden" approach where content is grouped by context.

```
articles/
├── README.md              # This file
├── ai/                    # Artificial Intelligence & ML
│   ├── Agents & Tooling
│   └── Strategy & Impact
├── cloud-native/          # Cloud-native architecture & patterns
├── design/                # UI/UX & Design trends
├── devops/                # Infrastructure, Git, CI/CD
│   ├── APIs & Specifications
│   ├── Authentication & Authorization
│   ├── Git & Version Control
│   ├── Observability & Operations
│   └── Platform & Scaling
├── distributed-systems/   # Distributed systems & messaging
├── environment/           # Climate & Environmental reports
├── frontend/              # Web development & frameworks
├── licensing/             # Open source licensing
├── philosophy/            # Tech philosophy & fables
│   ├── Career & Growth
│   ├── Critical Thinking & Emotions
│   ├── Information & Thought
│   ├── Personal & Wellness
│   └── Programming Philosophy
├── testing/               # QA & Testing strategies
├── tools/                 # Productivity & Developer tools
└── travel/                # Travel guides & experiences
```

## Digital Garden Approach

Unlike a traditional blog where posts are static and ordered by date, ThoughtsInWords follows the **Digital Garden** philosophy:

*   **Growth over time**: Articles are not "finished" upon publication. They are seeds that grow as new information is learned and added.
*   **Contextual Organization**: Content is linked and grouped by topic (tags) rather than purely by timestamps, making it easier to explore related ideas.
*   **Evolution**: Some notes may start as rough thoughts (seedlings) and evolve into comprehensive guides (evergreens).
*   **Interconnectivity**: Heavy use of internal linking connects disparate ideas, fostering a network of knowledge rather than a stream of updates.

## Getting Started

1. Navigate to the relevant tag folder (e.g., `articles/devops/`).
2. Create a new markdown file: `my-topic.md`.
3. Link it in the index below.
